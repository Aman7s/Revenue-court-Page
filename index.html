This website is about different types of Revenue Courts
Revenue courts are specialized courts or tribunals that handle disputes related to land revenue, agricultural land, tenancy, and property records. They are usually managed by government revenue or land administration officials rather than regular civil judges, and they commonly deal with issues such as land ownership records, boundary disputes, rent, taxes, and inheritance of agricultural property.
Different stages of courts.
  • A trial court is the first court where a case is heard, evidence is examined, and the initial judgment is given.
  • An appellate court reviews decisions of lower courts to determine whether legal errors were made in the original judgment.
  • An upper courts are the higher judicial authority that gives the interpretation of law and hears major appeals.
  • A supreme court is the highest court in a legal system that gives the final decision on important legal and constitutional matters.
